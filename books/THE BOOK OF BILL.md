#### My Story

Bill talks about the early days from when he was born and his 2D homeworld. He discusses his rare mutation that allowed him to see the third dimension and how he wanted to show it to his world. Although distorted, it can be seen that his attempts to show the 3rd dimension destroyed his world and killed everyone there. After escaping, he became the galactic overlord of the new dimension he went to the Nightmare Realm, a chaotic dimension between universes and built his crew. After many years of good times, his dimension began to collapse and he tried to find a new one to take over. After a deal with Time Baby falls apart, Bill sends him crashing into Earth, where he is frozen. He now needed someone to open the door to the dimension to let him in.

This leads him to the valley of Gravity Falls where he attempts to get a Shaman to build the first portal. After finding out what Bill truly was, he burned the portal and banned Bill from the valley with a curse and prophecy. Banished from Gravity Falls, Bill attempts to get his portal built with the help of various other humans. These include the ancient Egyptians, Aztecs and Easter Island. Eventually he tries to get a wizard in the dark ages to help who instead traps him. Bill's escape causes the start of Cipherstitions; history's first full blown Bill panic. Bill eventually returns to America to try and get humans there to build his portal from witches to the US government during the country's founding.

Eventually his attempts at getting to people in America leads to the creation of the Anti-Cipher Society in 1901. Founded by Thurburt Mudget Waxstaff III, he and various other victims of Bill try to defeat him, but are deemed a joke after trying to get funding to enclose America in a Bill-Hunting suit.

Seeing that one-on-one human interactions were not working, Bill tries to use animation to get to the minds of the world to build his portal. After that failed, Bill tries to see if nuclear testing could make his portal work, only to have him be captured by the government before escaping. Bill then tries music, computer technology and even collectable figurines to manipulate the masses to his cause. Nothing worked until one day, someone reverses the Shaman's curse and summons him back to Gravity Falls...a moment that makes him laugh so hard that even NASA and the Soviets picks it up. That person is Stanford Pines.

#### Journal 3: The Missing Pages

The pages follow Ford in his isolation in Gravity Falls and encounters with Bill convincing him to build the portal. It follows their early relationship and the ups and downs of it. One day, after seeing one of Stan's scams on TV, Bill advises Ford to forget the past and move on, revealing the only surviving piece of his dimension; a single speck of dust. When asked what destroyed his dimension, Bill replies that it was destroyed "by a monster." After offering to help him find and seek it out for revenge, Bill joylessly laughs and says, "Sixer, it would eat you alive."

Ford then discusses Christmas, when Fiddleford is home with his family, leaving Ford to try to spend it alone without Bill. After being rescued from Krampus by Fiddleford McGucket, who returned early due to issues with his wife, Ford makes it up to him by having a proper holiday party with him. However, Bill returns and after an argument, convinces Ford to be cautious of McGucket, planting in him the mindset to "TRUST NO ONE."

Ford eventually gets a warning in his dreams about Bill in an anagram that reads "I Grow Maddened," which arranges to say Weirdmageddon. Once realizing Bill's true intentions, he begins to consider destroying the journals and discovers evidence of Bill trying to reactivate the portal, such as injuring him while trying to open the door to the portal room. Ford then plans to rebuild an updated version of the Bill-Proof Suit that the Anti Cipher Society had tried to make over a century ago. However, Bill possesses corpses to try and stop him from gathering the supplies. After many attempts at stopping him, Ford awakes one morning on the roof of the shack to find a video tape Bill had made of various things he did to him while possessing him that night.

After warning him to not cross him again, Bill gives Ford 72 hours to restart the portal or his eyes will be stolen. Desperate, Ford plans out calling Stanley to help him out of this. This eventually will lead to the incident that leads Ford to be lost in the multiverse.

Having lost Ford, Bill is left without a new way into the dimension and eventually causes a drunken disturbance at an interdimensional restaurant. Eventually, he discusses how he did end up succeeding and tells the story of Weirdmageddon and eventual downfall. He then reveals his new plan.

While reading the book, Bill had gone inside the mind of the reader and rewired their brain to be compliant with his plan for revenge. He then lays out the following mission...

Go to Gravity Falls and shake his statue's hand.
Bill enters the reader's body and uses it to restart one of the many portals still around, specifically the dark ages one.
Bill reassembles himself and the reader gets their body back.
Weirdmageddon 2.0
Kill the Pines with sharp objects.
Fiesta Time.
You gonna be the king of the world HAHAHHAHAHA, with me obvious. (DUH)
Bill tries to convince the reader to help him, only for Stanford to come back to reveal his revelations about the Book of Bill: The book is Bill's last attempt at fighting his biggest fear of being forgotten. By reading the book, we give him attention, not power. By ignoring it, Bill is powerless. After passages from Mabel, Dipper and Stan telling Bill what they'll do to him if he ever comes back, Bill tries one last time to convince the reader to help him, only to realize the Pines' messages worked and he has once again been defeated.

#### Bill's Fate

A new poem from the Axolotl reveals Bill has been sent to a Neutral zone in Dimension 5150 called the Theraprism, which is outside of time. There, he is undergoing mandatory therapy to try and rehabilitate him. The Theraprism apologizes to the reader, saying Bill managed to contact them through an arts and crafts project where he made the book, which is a violation of their rules against outside contact. They give Bill 5 minutes to finish telling his story and then will confiscate his book.

Bill finally comes clean and admits he's stuck in the prism. He states that despite it all, he's not changed and never will. He wants someone to break him out through the help of the book, but in the end, we are just like the rest who refuse to help. Stating there will always be another, Bill severs the connection between him and us and reiterates that despite what anyone says, he does not need or miss anyone. He's fine, and someday, someone will let him out.

The book ends with a dark image of his statue somewhere in the woods of Gravity Falls, as its eye starts glowing blue.
