Bill Cipher - Trivia

Creation

Bill Cipher was created by Alex Hirsch as part of the mythology surrounding Gravity Falls.

Originally, Bill was intended to be a much smaller character before eventually becoming the series' primary antagonist.

Inspiration

Major inspirations include:

- The Eye of Providence
- Pyramid symbolism
- Conspiracy theories
- Secret societies
- Flatland by Edwin Abbott

Voice Actor

Bill Cipher is voiced by Alex Hirsch.

David Lynch was originally considered for the role before Hirsch ultimately voiced the character himself.

Name Origins

The name "Bill Cipher" likely references:

- Dollar bills
- Cryptographic ciphers
- The Eye of Providence
- Lucifer and demonic folklore

Hidden Appearances

Bill appears throughout Gravity Falls long before his first speaking appearance.

His image can be found:

- In the Mystery Shack
- In background objects
- In opening sequences
- In hidden visual clues

Cipher Hunt

After the series finale, Alex Hirsch organized a real-world treasure hunt known as the Cipher Hunt.

The hunt eventually led fans to Bill Cipher's statue.

The Book of Bill

The Book of Bill expanded Bill's backstory significantly.

Major revelations include:

- The destruction of Euclydia
- His relationship with the Axolotl
- His survival after Weirdmageddon
- His imprisonment within the Theraprism

Fun Facts

- Bill claims to be over one trillion years old.
- Bill responds to many variations of his name.
- Bill considers pain amusing.
- Bill enjoys silly straws.
- Bill's favorite activity is causing chaos.
- Bill was originally envisioned as green before becoming yellow.

References

Bill has been referenced across numerous Gravity Falls books, promotional materials, puzzles, cryptograms, and expanded-universe content.

He remains one of the most analyzed and discussed characters in the franchise.
