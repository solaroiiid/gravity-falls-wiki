The Nightmare Realm, also known as Dimension O, is a seething and intergalactic foam between dimensions where Bill Cipher resided after destroying his origin universe, the second dimension. He sought to escape by entering the world of Gravity Falls, Oregon in an event known as Weirdmageddon.

## History
At least one trillion years before the series within the second dimension, Bill Cipher "liberated" his world by plunging it into burning chaos, along with its inhabitants. He then proceeded to take over the empty Nightmare Realm as his own. He described the Nightmare Realm as "his own decaying dimension" in which he had "been trapped in for one trillion years", looking for a means to escape. The Nightmare Realm's lack of constant physics makes it filled with pure chaos. When Stan Pines finally activated the Interdimensional Portal in an attempt to save his brother Ford, it created an Interdimensional rift. Bill later used this to create a gateway between Dimension 46'\ (referring to where Gravity Falls is) and the Nightmare Realm.

In "The Last Mabelcorn," Bill appears within Stanford's dreams to haunt him, and warn him of the upcoming inevitable events. While doing so, he gives Ford a glimpse of the Nightmare Realm by shattering the rift. Later, Bill, who is in the Realm, watches Ford and his great-nephew, Dipper, successfully establishing a shield around the Mystery Shack and starts to choose other people to resume his plan.

In "Dipper and Mabel vs. the Future," Bill has possessed the body of Blendin Blandin in order to retrieve the rift from Mabel Pines, which he uses to create the previously prophesied gateway to the Nightmare Realm.

In "Weirdmageddon Part 1," Bill uses the newly created portal in the sky to bring forth his gang of nightmare monsters and inter-dimensional criminals from the Nightmare Realm.

In "Weirdmageddon 2: Escape From Reality," the Nightmare Realm is still visible through the tear in the sky above Gravity Falls created by Bill. However, Bill and his gang are unable to leave Gravity Falls; according to Ford, natural law of weirdness magnetism of the town contains Bill's weirdness, and an equation can disable the barrier.

In "Weirdmageddon 3: Take Back The Falls," when Bill was destroyed through the Pines twins' cunning, his minions were sucked back into the Nightmare Realm, never to be seen again.

In Gravity Falls: Lost Legends, the Nightmare Realm is the major settlement for the tale "Don't Dimension It!", Mabel accidentally falls through one of the remaining rifts created by the Rift, after the events of the finale, thanks to a Hand Monstrosity (previously mentioned in Journal 3). She manages to evade it, but falls through other dimensions, among them the place where Bill and his forces were imprisoned (we see Keyhole and 8 Ball once again staring angrily to a statue of Bill, briefly), until reaching MAB-3L. Later in the tale, Stan and Ford come to the Nightmare Realm looking for Mabel, until Ford decides to use a beacon to call a samaritan, who doesn't allow them to take a ride, due Ford's criminal record. So Stan persuades him to let them enter while apprehending Ford. The Nightmare Realm is seen once again after Anti-Mabel's defeat, when the other Mabels give Stan, Ford and Mabel a ride to the rift, before returning to their respective dimensions.

## Appearance
The Nightmare Realm, from what is shown, is a somewhat surreal unstable void filled with swirling shades of dark purple, red and yellow almost like a lava lamp. In the real-life canonical Journal 3, it says that the Realm is littered with asteroids and has a "smell of burnt hair."

In Gravity Falls: Lost Legends, the Nightmare Realm looks like space, filled with stars and other celestial bodies and littered with Weirdness Bubbles. It seems that the Nightmare Realm is split in zones, because the zone where 8 Ball and Keyhole were (and subsequently all of Bill's Henchmaniacs are), resembled more the Nightmare Realm that appears in the series, described above.

## Inhabitants
The Nightmare Realm has been used as a home for some of the worst criminals of the Multiverse, including Bill and his Henchmaniacs (such as 8 Ball, Amorphous Shape, Hectorgon, Keyhole, Kryptos, Paci-Fire, Teeth, Pyronica, Lava Lamp, The Horrifying Sweaty One-Armed Monstrosity, Creature with 88 different faces, and Zanthar) however, due to it connecting to many other universes, beings from more dimensions have found themselves stuck in the Nightmare Realm with no means of escape. These include a group of asteroid miners Ford encountered after first entering the Realm. In Gravity Falls: Lost Legends, for what it seems the Nightmare Realm is split in different zones, the miners could had gotten stuck in another zone. Other inhabitants include a Chtulu offspring and the Hand Monstrosity.
