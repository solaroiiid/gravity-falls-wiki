
Euclydia (or the "second dimension") is the name of Bill Cipher's origin dimension. Inhabited by beings known as Euclideans, it was destroyed almost exactly one trillion years ago through unknown means by Bill.

## History
**Background**
While the dimension is not shown, Euclydia is said to be a two-dimensional realm, one that three-dimensional minds couldn't process. Its inhabitants take the form of sentient two-dimensional shapes that bump around endlessly within; their line of scope limited to a flat line of sight. Although subject to speculation due to Bill's questionable reliability as a narrator, Bill claimed that he was beloved by everyone, so much that the mayor declared his birthday a national holiday, gave out free knives and declared him the "best baby of all time".

However, it is implied that Bill's claims were false, as Bill despised living there. Unlike his people, Bill had a rare mutation that allowed him to see the third dimension, but as no one else had it; they could not understand what he was referring to by saying there was a direction known as "up". Embittered by this and being the only one able to see the stars in a dimension where talk of a third one was illegal, Bill decided to "free" his people from their delusions and make them understand.

**Euclidean Massacre**
To allow his dimension to see the third dimension, Bill decided to put on a show to show everyone what they were missing. However, the plan went horribly wrong. Based on Bill's recount, although incomplete, what can be read is that he could hear screams getting louder and louder, as blood was everywhere. Bill mentioned his hands began shaking as he realized he could not undo what had happened and after the last one of his dimension takes a breath (with a mention of episodes of Family Matters, with some odd relation to mandibles), Bill was left alone, covered in blood and alone in the universe. Although stating that he believes the plan was a success and liberated his dimension; in reality, Bill, in his attempt to show his people the third dimension, accidentally destroyed them all and his world, the event becoming known as the Euclidean Massacre. Bill then escaped to another dimension, the Nightmare Realm, and began his tenure of wreaking transdimensional havoc.

**Legacy**
Perhaps due to the trauma and his initial remorse at the event, wanting to deny what he did or the effect the act had on him, Bill was unable to remember the full details of that day and continued to deny Euclydia's destruction, making it unclear whether he destroyed his dimension knowingly or by mistake. When being hunted down by Time Baby, he was listed as the sole survivor of the massacre, implying he is the last remaining being from the dimension.[3]

While manipulating Ford Pines to build his portal and helping him overcome his anger about seeing a con ad by Stan on television, Bill told him to let go of the past and revealed the sole surviving piece of his dimension; a single speck of dust. When asked what destroyed it, Bill, telling a half-truth, claimed it was destroyed "by a monster". After Ford offered to help him find and seek the monster out for revenge, Bill joylessly laughed and stated it would "eat him alive".[4]

One night, after losing Ford, Bill caused a scene at an interdimensional tavern and drunkenly tried looking for his mother, telling her he would be back home from school soon and to cut the crust off his sandwiches. He then asked where she went before he was arrested by interdimensional police.[5]

Ford later learned about Euclydia from The Oracle, a being who knew Bill Cipher. While recovering from having a metal plate put in his head to protect his mind from Bill, he learned that Bill's lust for power caused him to destroy his own dimension, parents and everyone he knew. She spoke of him without anger, but in a way that implied she wanted to see him defeated. It later was revealed she once was part of Bill's crew.[1]

Bill first alludes to Euclydia in "Weirdmageddon 3: Take Back The Falls" while showing off his physical form and telling Ford that his tight chains are nothing compared to his home. He says it was full of "flat minds in a flat world with flat dreams." He reiterates the lie that he liberated his dimension and wants to do the same to Ford's. While he says this, he appears to show concern as his eye shows flames and the sounds of screams.

In the sole canon part of the book Dipper and Mabel and the Curse of the Time Pirates' Treasure!, a poem by the Axolotl further adds that Bill saw his own dimension burn and despite missing home, he can't return. His statement, "blame the arson for the fire," hints that his actions burned it.

**Trivia**
The term "Euclidean" refers to a form of geometry attributed to the ancient Greek mathematician Euclid. To put it simply, it is the study of geometrical shapes (plane and solid) and figures based on different axioms and theorems.
Prior to 2024, the dimension went unnamed in Gravity Falls and its related material. Although not named in The Book of Bill, a Morse code message heard in the song "Weirdmageddon Pt. 1A" from the Lofi: Gravity Falls album does mention it.
".-. . -- . -- -... . .-./. ..- -.-. .-.. -.-- -.. .. .-". When decoded from morse code , it says, "REMEMBER EUCLYDIA".
Its name was alluded to in a cipher on Bill's wanted poster in the book, and eventually confirmed in one of the messages of This Is Not A Website Dot Com.
When "Euclydia" is typed in the computer, the monitor will say "Dimension not found".
According to Bill, the only way to process what his dimension was like would be if one chugged expired milk while looking through a kaleidoscope.
Euclydia is likely inspired by the Flatland, a satirical science fiction novella written by Edwin Abbott Abbott.
It is unknown if previous beings from Euclydia had the same mutation Bill did that allowed him to view the third dimension.
It similarly is unknown what the life expectancy of Euclideans is and if Bill's long existence is attributed to that or his mutation. In either case, he is likely the longest living Euclidean.
In the tie-in website to The Book of Bill called This Is Not A Website Dot Com, several words inputted into it mention or reference Euclydia and the massacre.
